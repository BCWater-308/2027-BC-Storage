Method, in brief

Per polygon: ΔStoragep,y = (GWEp,y − GWEp,baseline) × Syp × Areap. GWEp,y is the polygon's 2027 GWL RMS well's spring composite (March mean for SWN-named wells), Good-quality DWR records only. Each polygon is anchored to WY 1999 if it has a Good spring composite that year; otherwise to the polygon's first observation after 1999. We then take the per-polygon cumulative storage time series, compute year-over-year deltas (distributing multi-year DWR gaps evenly), and bucket each year by its official Sacramento Valley Index water-year type.

Specific yield is polygon-by-polygon, derived from DWR's SVSim Texture Data (Sacramento Valley Simulation Model v1.0). Coarse-grained sediments → Sy = 0.15, fine-grained → Sy = 0.05, area-weighted by borehole lithology in the 0–500 ft below ground surface analysis window. Polygon Sy values range 0.0594 to 0.1172; basin area-weighted mean ≈ 0.087.

3 polygons (23N02W25C001M, 23N01E29P002M, 21N01E25K001M) have insufficient SVSim borehole coverage and use the basin area-weighted mean as a Sy fallback. Flagged with "(mean)" in the table.

Year-type classification uses DWR's Sacramento Valley Index (Northern Sierra 8-Station Index):

  • Wet: 1999, 2006, 2011, 2017, 2019, 2023, 2025 (7 years)
  • Above Normal: 2000, 2003, 2005, 2024 (4 years)
  • Below Normal: 2004, 2010, 2012, 2016, 2018 (5 years)
  • Dry: 2001, 2002, 2007, 2009, 2013, 2020 (6 years)
  • Critical: 2008, 2014, 2015, 2021, 2022 (5 years)

When and where the basin loses water

Figure 1. Sum across all 28 polygons, gap-attributed by year, bucketed by official Sacramento Valley Index water-year type. Critical years alone average 45,977 AF/yr of loss — about 1.3× the per-year loss rate of Dry years.
Figure 2. Basin cumulative ΔStorage. Solid blue line = observed (each polygon contributes only years its RMS well actually measured). Dashed purple line = year-type-weighted normalized (corrects for late-baseline drag — see callout below).
Late-baseline drag and the year-type-weighted normalization. Of the 28 polygons, only 10 have a Good March measurement in WY 1999. The other 18 baseline later — between 2000 and 2019 — because their 2027 GWL RMS well wasn't measured in 1999. Late-baseline polygons cannot register their pre-baseline drawdown, so the observed basin cumulative (-145,732 AF through 2025) understates what the basin would show if every polygon had a full record.

The year-type-weighted normalized series corrects this. For each polygon, we compute its average ΔStorage per Sacramento Valley Index year type using only its own observations. We then synthesize what that polygon would have contributed across the full WY 1999–2025 record by applying its per-type rates to the basin's actual year-type mix (6 Wet, 4 AN, 5 BN, 6 Dry, 5 Critical = 26 transition years). Summed across all 28 polygons, that gives the normalized basin total: -189,209 AF through 2025 — an avg loss rate of -7,277 AF/yr, vs. the observed 6,322 AF/yr.

The 2042 sustainability target — hold the line, with the project portfolio

The Vina Subbasin has lost storage across the WY 1999–2025 record through 5 Critical years, 6 Dry years, 5 Below-Normal years, and a long-term decline rate of about 0.07%/yr. The basin's historic low was the WY 2022 trough at roughly 285,567 AF below baseline — and even at that low, no SGMA sustainability indicator registered an undesirable result.

The framing here is therefore not "recover to the WY 1999 baseline." The operational target is: GWE at each Groundwater Level RMS well stays at or above its WY 2022 level going forward. MTs for the GSP would be set lower than WY 2022, creating operational range and margin for an unforeseen 7–10 year drought, similar to what the subbasin experienced between 1985 and 1994. The portfolio's job is to ensure the next major drought doesn't push observed GWE below WY 2022.
